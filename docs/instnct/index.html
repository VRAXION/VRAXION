Founder note / VRAXION

A local-first path toward decentralized intelligence.

VRAXION is the project identity named by Daniel Kenessy. Formal trademark registration is not claimed here; the VRAXION names, logos, icons, and trade dress are reserved where applicable under the public mark boundary.

Long horizon

Continuity, autonomy, diversity.

The long-term aim is to help preserve and extend the continuity of intelligent life and its future successors, while protecting autonomy, diversity, and the possibility of better futures.

Near mission

Affordable local intelligence.

The immediate path starts with a public, free-to-download demo aimed at people who cannot depend on expensive subscriptions or constant cloud access.

Build status

Small team, public boundary.

This is an independent founder-built project. Progress is intentionally bounded, public-facing claims are conservative, and stabilization comes before larger capability promises.

Next step

Ship, listen, fix.

The first practical goal is to release the free demo, verify that it runs reliably across real machines, then fix the failures that users actually report.

Roadmap

Larger local releases, curated skills, test-time learning.

Once the free path is stable, the roadmap moves toward larger local releases, creative and larger-scale reasoning surfaces, optional learning during inference, and curated skill libraries that could be distributed through trusted institutions.

Licensing intent

Sustainable first, open later.

The long-term hope is to make more of the system open source once there is enough sustainable revenue to protect the work, fund development, and avoid losing the project before it can mature.
