# VRAXION Commercial Use Guide

This guide explains the repository license in plain language. It is not legal
advice, and it does not replace the controlling terms in the root
[`LICENSE`](../LICENSE).

VRAXION is released under the **VRAXION Community Source License 1.0**. This is
a custom source-available community license, not an OSI-approved open source
license.

## Free community use

No royalty is owed for uses that do not monetize third-party access to
VRAXION-powered functionality.

Examples:

- personal use;
- research;
- education;
- nonprofit work;
- community forks;
- self-hosting;
- benchmarking and evaluation;
- free public demos with no monetization;
- internal company use where no third party is charged for access to
  VRAXION-powered functionality.

## Royalty use

Royalty is owed when revenue or other commercial consideration is received in
exchange for third-party access to VRAXION-powered functionality.

Examples:

- paid hosted services;
- paid APIs;
- paid SaaS or subscriptions;
- paid managed deployments;
- paid enterprise access;
- paid applications where VRAXION-powered functionality is a material feature;
- resale, white-label, or sublicensed access;
- ad-supported, sponsored, affiliate, lead-generation, data-resale, or similar
  monetized access.

The royalty is **19% of Attributable Net Revenue** from Royalty Use.

Default allocation:

- **1% Founder Allocation**
- **18% VRAXION Forever Prize Allocation**

After a Founder Redirect Event, the Founder Allocation redirects to the Prize,
so the full **19%** goes to the **VRAXION Forever Prize Allocation**.

The total royalty is always **19%**, not 20%.

## What counts as VRAXION-powered functionality?

The license covers functionality materially enabled by the Project Materials:
source code, scripts, runtime, probes, model/checkpoint artifacts, documentation,
evaluation harnesses, and derivatives or adaptations of those materials.

A clean-room implementation that does not copy, adapt, use, link to, derive
from, or incorporate Project Materials is not VRAXION-powered merely because it
uses a similar general idea. Public releases, dated artifacts, and provenance
records still matter because they help establish what was disclosed and when.
