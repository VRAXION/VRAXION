# VRAXION Forever Prize Charter

Version: 1.0
Effective date: 2026-06-13

## 1. Purpose

The VRAXION Forever Prize exists to support the long-term survival, continuity, safety, dignity, diversity, and flourishing of consciousness and conscious-capable substrates.

Short objective:

> Ensure consciousness in the universe and beyond. End of duty: Forever.

Operational objective:

> Preserve, protect, expand, and responsibly sustain consciousness, subjective experience, and conscious-capable substrates across the longest feasible time horizons, including biological, digital, hybrid, and future substrates.

## 2. Funding source

The Prize is funded by the VRAXION royalty allocation:

- 18 percentage points of Attributable Net Revenue before a Founder Redirect Event;
- 19 percentage points of Attributable Net Revenue after a Founder Redirect Event.

The Founder Allocation is 1 percentage point before redirect.

After a Founder Redirect Event, the Founder Allocation redirects to the Prize, so the full 19% royalty funds the Prize.

## 3. Eligible recipients

Recipients may include:

- individuals;
- independent researchers;
- open research communities;
- universities;
- nonprofits;
- public-interest labs;
- companies;
- AI labs;
- safety labs;
- infrastructure projects;
- archival projects;
- prize competitions;
- emergency resilience projects.

Commercial entities may receive awards only when the award is milestone-based and advances the Prize purpose.

## 4. Example funding categories

The Prize may fund:

- consciousness science;
- synthetic consciousness evaluation;
- animal welfare and non-human consciousness research;
- AI welfare research;
- AI safety and alignment;
- mechanistic interpretability;
- deception and shutdown-risk evaluation;
- resilient civilization infrastructure;
- open scientific archives;
- safe compute access for small labs;
- digital preservation;
- disaster recovery;
- biological and ecological continuity;
- space settlement risk reduction;
- long-horizon governance;
- public-interest benchmarks and evaluations.

## 5. AI Allocator role

A VRAXION AI system may act as an evaluator and allocator by:

- scoring proposals;
- detecting conflicts of interest;
- checking evidence quality;
- estimating long-term value;
- ranking grants;
- generating rationales;
- auditing outcomes;
- recommending continuation, pause, or clawback.

Unless legally authorized, the AI Allocator does not directly own assets, sign contracts, or execute payments. A legally recognized steward, fiduciary, foundation, trust, or entity executes payments.

## 6. Guardrails

Prize distributions should avoid:

- unchecked capability acceleration without safety benefit;
- concentrated control over consciousness-related infrastructure;
- secret milestones where public audit is feasible;
- conflicts of interest;
- self-dealing;
- political capture;
- irreversible deployment of high-risk systems without oversight;
- grants that increase existential or catastrophic risk more than they reduce it.

## 7. Distribution method

Preferred distribution style:

- milestone-based grants;
- public proposal summaries;
- public award rationales;
- independent review for large grants;
- outcome reporting;
- periodic red-team review;
- reserve/endowment preservation for long-horizon continuity.

## 8. Endowment principle

Because the objective is Forever, the Prize should preserve a long-term capital base where feasible.

Recommended default:

- spend no more than 3% to 5% of long-term endowment assets per year;
- reserve emergency funds for civilization-scale or consciousness-scale risk;
- publish yearly reports.

## 9. Founder redirect

A Founder Redirect Event causes the Founder Allocation to become part of the Prize allocation.

A valid redirect may be triggered by:

- Daniel Kenessy's death;
- written founder instruction;
- notarized instruction;
- cryptographically signed public instruction;
- will, trust, foundation document, or other legally effective instrument;
- official VRAXION repository notice stating that redirect occurred.

## 10. OpenAI and other major labs

The Prize may fund OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, Meta, xAI, Mistral, academic labs, independent labs, or any other organization if the award clearly advances the Prize purpose.

No recipient receives funds merely because of brand, scale, or prestige.

Large labs should generally receive milestone-based awards with public evidence, independent audit, or matching-fund requirements.
